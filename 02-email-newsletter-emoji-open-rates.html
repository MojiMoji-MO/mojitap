Among a black-and-white list of text-only emails, colorful emojis act as powerful visual anchors that force users to stop scrolling. According to data analysis by Mailchimp, emails using appropriate emojis have an open rate that is on average 56% higher than plain emails. Furthermore, in mobile environments, subject lines are truncated. Emojis maximize spatial efficiency by compressing long words into an intuitive symbol.

3. Fatal Mistake: How to Avoid Spam Filters

Emojis are excellent weapons, but misusing them can send your emails straight to the 'Spam' folder instead of the inbox. There are 3 ironclad rules to pass the spam filters of major email clients like Google (Gmail).

Limit the Count: Restrict emojis to 1-2 per subject line. More triggers spam bots.
Check Compatibility: Some emojis may break into empty boxes depending on OS. Use universal ones.
Don't Substitute Words: Keep the text intact and place the emoji at the end for screen-reader alignment.
